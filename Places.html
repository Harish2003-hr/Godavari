ARAKU VALLEY

Araku Valley is a picturesque hill station located in the Alluri Sitharama Raju district of Andhra Pradesh, near the Odisha border. Nestled in the Eastern Ghats, it is known for its lush green landscapes, waterfalls, tribal culture, and pleasant climate.The valley is famous for Araku Coffee, grown organically by tribal farmers, and for attractions like Borra Caves, Padmapuram Gardens, and Dumbriguda Waterfalls. It's a popular weekend getaway from Visakhapatnam, connected by a scenic train route that passes through tunnels and valleys. Araku is not only a natural paradise but also a cultural hub, offering a glimpse into the life and traditions of the indigenous tribes.



TIRUPATI

Tirupati is a sacred pilgrimage city located in the Tirupati district of Andhra Pradesh, known for the world-famous Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple at Tirumala. It is one of the most visited religious destinations in the world, attracting millions of devotees every year. The temple is situated on the Seven Hills (Saptagiri) and is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Tirupati is also known for its spiritual atmosphere, rich traditions, and cultural heritage. Apart from the temple, visitors also explore attractions like Kapila Theertham, Sri Padmavathi Temple, and Silathoranam.



AMARAVATHI

Amaravathi is an ancient and culturally significant town located on the banks of the Krishna River in Andhra Pradesh. It is known for its historical importance as a former capital of the Satavahana dynasty and as a prominent Buddhist center. The famous Amaravathi Stupa, built over 2,000 years ago, is a major attraction and an important Buddhist pilgrimage site. In recent years, Amaravathi has been proposed as the capital region for Andhra Pradesh, symbolizing modern growth blended with heritage. The town is also home to the Amaralingeswara Swamy Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, adding to its spiritual significance.



CORINGA WILDLIFE SANCTUARY, KAKINADA

Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary is located near Kakinada in the East Godavari district of Andhra Pradesh. It is the second-largest stretch of mangrove forests in India and is known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. The sanctuary is home to over 120 species of birds, including endangered ones like the white-backed vulture and long-billed vulture. It also shelters animals like jackals, fishing cats, otters, and various species of crabs and fish. Coringa is an ideal spot for nature lovers and bird watchers, with wooden walkways allowing visitors to explore the lush mangrove ecosystem.
