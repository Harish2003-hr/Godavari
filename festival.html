PONGAL

Pongal is a popular harvest festival celebrated mainly in Tamil Nadu and parts of South India. It is dedicated to the Sun God and marks the beginning of the harvest season. The festival usually lasts four days and is celebrated with traditional rituals, decorations, and feasts. A special dish called "Pongal," made of rice and jaggery, is cooked and offered in gratitude. It reflects joy, prosperity, and a deep connection to nature and farming life.



UGADI

Ugadi is the New Year festival celebrated in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. It marks the beginning of the Telugu and Kannada lunar calendars, usually falling in March or April.The festival symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and prosperity. People clean their homes, wear new clothes, decorate with mango leaves, and prepare special dishes like Ugadi Pachadi. Ugadi Pachadi, a mixture of six tastes, reflects the different emotions of life—sweet, sour, bitter, and more.



DUSSEHRA

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a major Hindu festival celebrated across India. It marks the victory of good over evil, symbolized by Lord Rama’s triumph over the demon king Ravana. In many places, large effigies of Ravana are burned to celebrate this victory. It also signifies the end of the nine-day Navaratri festival dedicated to Goddess Durga. People celebrate with cultural performances, processions, and festive meals, spreading the message of righteousness and courage.



VINAYAKA CHAVITHI

Vinayaka Chavithi, also known as Ganesh Chaturthi, is a popular Hindu festival celebrated to honor Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles. It usually falls in August or September and is celebrated with great devotion, especially in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Maharashtra. People install clay idols of Lord Ganesha in homes and public pandals, and offer prayers, sweets like modaks, and perform aarti. The festival lasts for 1 to 11 days, ending with the immersion of idols in water bodies. It symbolizes wisdom, prosperity, and the removal of negativity from life.
