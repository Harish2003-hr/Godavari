ANNAVARAM PRASADHAM

Annavaram Prasadam is the sacred offering given at the famous Sri Veera Venkata Satyanarayana Swamy Temple in Annavaram, Andhra Pradesh. The prasadam is a unique and delicious sweet called “Sakinala Prasadam” or “Halwa prasadam,” made from wheat flour, jaggery, ghee, and cardamom. It is prepared with great devotion and offered to the deity during the Satyanarayana Swamy Vratham. Devotees believe the prasadam brings peace, prosperity, and blessings to their homes. The temple is visited by thousands daily, and the prasadam is an essential part of the divine experience.



TIRUPATI LADDU

The Tirupati Laddu is a world-famous prasadam offered at the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple in Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh. It is made from high-quality ingredients like gram flour, sugar, ghee, cardamom, and dry fruits, prepared in the temple’s sacred kitchen known as the Potu. The laddu has a unique taste and divine significance, believed to carry the blessings of Lord Venkateswara. It holds a Geographical Indication (GI) tag, protecting its authenticity and traditional preparation. Millions of devotees consider receiving the Tirupati Laddu a holy and cherished part of their pilgrimage.



RAVULAPALEM KUNDA(POT) BIRYANI

Hailing from the small town of Ravulapalem in East Godavari, this “kunda” biryani began around 2005 by a local family cooking just ten clay pots for highway travelers—but quickly became legendary for its taste.Prepared in individual clay pots ("kunda" or matka), rice, marinated chicken or mutton, boiled eggs, aromatic spices, yoghurt, and herbs are layered and sealed—often with foil—then cooked over high flame and finished on simmer for a rich, dum-style biryani.The pot gives it extra depth—a tender, spicy, moist biryani with golden rice and whole egg garnish. Many food lovers now travel to Ravulapalem just for this rustic biryani experience .



ATREYYAPURAM PUTHAREKULU

Putharekulu—literally "coated sheets" in Telugu—is often called the “paper sweet.” It’s crafted from wafer-thin rice flour sheets layered with fillings like jaggery or sugar and fragrant ghee, then expertly folded or rolled.The sweet is a culinary specialty of Atreyapuram village in Konaseema district, known for its meticulous, labor-intensive craft passed down through generations.Popular variants include Bellam (jaggery), Sugar, and Dry-Fruit Putharekulu; there are also modern versions with chocolate or sugar-free options.
